# Master Dungeon Art Direction

## Name

**Abyssal Necropolis**

## Narrative premise

A gate opens into the buried capital of a dead monarch. The city was constructed around a vertical royal road, later converted into an ossuary, foundry, prison, and ritual sepulcher. Its defenders did not flee; they were bound into the architecture and continue guarding the Heart Vault.

The environment tells a chronological story:

1. hunters enter through a recently awakened gate chamber;
2. watcher statues and a broken bridge reveal the scale of the buried city;
3. ossuary and foundry wings show how the dead kingdom processed bodies and weapons;
4. the central plaza reveals the ruined civic core;
5. basilica, prison, and archive show the monarch's control system;
6. the Sunken Court and sepulcher expose the supernatural corruption at the center.

## Visual identity

A monumental subterranean gothic kingdom shaped by deepslate, blackstone, basalt, bone, cyan gate energy, soul light, and restrained gold. The map must read as one civilization with six functional districts, not six unrelated themes.

## Architectural influences

- subterranean cathedral proportions
- fortified necropolis walls
- ribbed vaults and procession halls
- industrial basalt foundries
- civic plazas with a central monument
- prison galleries and drowned archives
- royal sepulcher and throne arena

## Palette

### Primary

- deepslate
- deepslate bricks and tiles
- polished blackstone bricks
- blackstone
- basalt and polished basalt

### Secondary

- polished deepslate
- tuff
- cracked deepslate bricks
- smooth stone
- polished andesite

### Accents

- cyan concrete and sea lanterns for gate energy
- bone blocks for the ossuary
- magma and shroomlight for the foundry
- gilded blackstone and gold for royal hierarchy
- sculk for deep corruption
- obsidian for the sepulcher

### Lighting

- sea lantern: gate and climax
- soul lantern: civic/necropolis route
- shroomlight: industrial heat
- magma: hazards and foundry channels
- sparse hidden light: navigation continuity

## Shape language

- Gate Descent: tall ribs, clean axial framing, cyan horizontal bands.
- Ossuary Ward: narrow chapels, repeating bone bays, compressed passages.
- Abyssal Foundry: heavy columns, tanks, channels, industrial asymmetry.
- Buried Kingdom: broad plaza, civic symmetry broken by collapse, central vertical monument.
- Lower Catacombs: descending galleries, cages, pits, shelves, water-worn asymmetry.
- Monarch's Sepulcher: oversized black masses, gold-lined axes, elevated throne silhouette.

## Recurring motifs

- paired guardian columns
- seven-block architectural rhythm with deliberate interruptions
- central eye/heart shapes in floors and ceilings
- framed transitions rather than bare holes
- gold only on royal or reward-critical structures
- cyan energy only at gate-linked spaces
- sculk growth increasing with depth

## Landmark concepts

- Gate Hall energy crown
- Watcher Gallery statues
- Broken Bridge reveal
- Ossuary nave bone wall
- Foundry furnace array
- Buried Kingdom central monument
- Monarch Basilica dais
- Chained Prison gallery
- Drowned Archive stacks
- Sunken Court corruption ring
- Abyssal throne and Heart Vault

## Environmental hazards

- protected magma channels in the foundry
- broken bridge edges with safe lower containment
- controlled elevation changes in combat rooms
- prison and court choke points
- final-arena elevation rings

Hazards must be readable, bounded, and never part of the safe arrival point.

## Enemy atmosphere

Early enemies feel like gate scouts. Mid-dungeon enemies become organized wardens and foundry overseers. Deep enemies are shadow-bound guardians. The boss is the **Abyssal Monarch**, a heavy, telegraphed ruler encounter supported by shadow guardians.

## Audio atmosphere

- portal ambience and ranked gate effects at entry
- soul-fire and low industrial ambience in deep regions
- raid-horn objective starts
- ravager roars and telegraphed boss impacts
- restrained sound use so landmarks remain legible

## Final arena

The final arena is 89 blocks wide, 40 blocks deep, and up to 32 blocks tall. It uses an axial entrance, multiple floor elevations, broad lateral combat routes, an elevated throne landmark, blackstone/obsidian massing, gold focal trim, and sea-lantern contrast. The reward vault sits in a side wing opened after the boss.

## Cohesion rules

- No high-frequency checkerboard palettes.
- No random props.
- Every major room receives a unique silhouette.
- Gold indicates monarchy, objective, or reward.
- Cyan indicates gate influence.
- Heat colors stay in the foundry unless used as controlled foreshadowing.
- Sculk density increases with depth.
- Cracking and rubble follow collapse lines, not uniform scatter.
- Ceiling detail is mandatory in every major room.
